Executive Summary — FY2021–TTM (Mar 2026)

06 — Synthesis

Key Findings

  • Growth: Revenue CAGR of 31.39% (FY2021–FY2025); growth decelerated from a pandemic-recovery-driven 82.62% (FY2022) to a steadier ~17–18% band in FY2023–TTM.
  • Margins: GPM has been range-bound (~38–46%), while OPM and NPM expanded substantially — operating margin from -21.97% (FY2021) to 11.66% (TTM), profit margin from -3.05% to a peak of 22.47% (FY2024).
  • Cash generation: FCF turned positive in FY2022 and scaled to $9.76B in FY2025; FCF margin rose from -4.26% to 18.77%. Growth is now decelerating (TTM growth of just 0.37%) as the base matures.
  • FCF vs. revenue growth: FCF grew far faster than revenue through FY2023–FY2024 off a low/negative base, but has now converged with — and in the TTM period fallen behind — revenue growth, signaling that margin expansion is maturing.
  • Capital allocation: Shift from acquisitions (notably the $2.3B FY2021 outlay) toward large-scale buybacks ($6.5B FY2025, $7.7B TTM). No dividend.
  • Valuation: Current PE (17.39x) is near the median of the profitable-year range and well below the average, which is skewed by FY2023's earnings trough.

07 — Caveats

Risks & Data Limitations

  • Only three of the five fiscal years (FY2023–FY2025) plus TTM/Current have meaningful PE and earnings-yield readings; FY2021–FY2022 losses make those years N/M, narrowing the historical valuation sample used for averages/medians.
  • Net income includes minority interest and has been affected by large non-operating items (equity-investment marks, tax valuation allowance releases) in several years, which can distort period-over-period comparisons versus core operating performance.
  • Uber has ongoing large-scale corporate activity (e.g., a reported pursuit of Delivery Hero) not reflected in the historical financials above, which could affect future capital allocation and consolidated results.
  • Data sourced from StockAnalysis.com (Fiscal.ai), last updated May 6, 2026 for financials/cash-flow/ratios tables; some later developments (e.g., pending M&A) are not yet reflected in these figures.

08 — Close

Conclusion

Uber's five-year financial arc is one of a business that has converted rapid, capital-intensive growth into a high-margin, cash-generative model: revenue nearly tripled, operating and net margins moved from deeply negative to solidly positive, and free cash flow went from a $743M outflow to nearly $9.8B. Growth has normalized into the high-teens range, and the capital allocation focus has shifted decisively to share repurchases. At a PE near the middle of its (short) history of profitable years, the stock does not appear expensive relative to its own recent trading range, though the earnings base still carries some volatility from non-operating items worth monitoring.
